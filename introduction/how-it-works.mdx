Velum has three actions. Everything else is built on them.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Shield — make an asset private">
    You deposit an asset (ETH, a stablecoin, any supported token) into the Velum
    pool. In the same transaction you hand the pool a single **commitment** — a
    hash computed in your browser from two secrets only you know. The asset goes
    in; the public link to you is severed. You keep an **Access Key** that lets
    you spend it later.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Move — send or request privately">
    A payment request is just your commitment in a link. Whoever opens it deposits
    to *that* commitment, so the funds arrive **already shielded** — no account,
    and no Velum server ever sits in the path.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Unshield — cash out to any wallet">
    To withdraw, your browser builds a **zero-knowledge proof** that your
    commitment is one of the deposits in the pool — without revealing *which* one.
    The pool checks the proof and pays out to any address you choose. The link
    between where the money came from and where it went is broken.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## The mechanism, one level deeper

Every deposit becomes a leaf in an on-chain **Merkle tree**. The leaf is a hash:

```
handle = Poseidon(nullifier, secret)          // computed in your browser
leaf   = Poseidon(amount, token, handle)      // built by the contract
```

The two secrets — `nullifier` and `secret` — are yours alone and live only in your Access Key. The contract builds the leaf itself from the *real* amount and token, so a deposit can never lie about what it put in.

When you withdraw, you prove in zero knowledge that:

1. You know the `nullifier` and `secret` behind **some** leaf in the tree, and
2. That leaf commits to the amount and token you're withdrawing.

The proof reveals a **nullifier hash** — a one-way fingerprint that marks the note as spent so it can't be withdrawn twice — but it never reveals *which* leaf you spent. To an observer, the withdrawal could have come from any deposit in the tree. That set of indistinguishable deposits is your **anonymity set**.

<Info>
  No proof is required to **shield** — depositing is a single, cheap call. The
  zero-knowledge proof is only needed to **unshield**, and it's generated
  entirely in your browser (~30s). Your secrets never leave your device.
</Info>

## What breaks the link — and what doesn't

The magic is the *unlinkability* between deposit and withdrawal. But privacy is a system, not a single trick. Three things have to hold:

- **A relayer submits your withdrawal**, so your own wallet doesn't sign it. See [Cash out](/using-velum/cash-out).
- **The recipient is a fresh address** with no prior link to your deposit wallet.
- **The anonymity set is large enough** that "which deposit?" is genuinely unanswerable.

Get any of these wrong and the cryptography can't save you. We spell out exactly how in [The privacy model](/introduction/privacy-model) — read it before you rely on Velum for anything real.
