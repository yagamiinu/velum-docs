## The most public money in history

Money is going on-chain — not as an experiment anymore, but as infrastructure. Salaries, savings, settlements, company treasuries: the things people used to keep behind bank walls now live on open ledgers.

But here's what the pitch decks leave out: **every one of those transactions is public. Forever.**

- Anyone can pull up your wallet and read your entire net worth.
- Every payment permanently links you to whoever you paid.
- Businesses can't move a dollar without broadcasting payroll, runway, and strategy to competitors.
- And none of it ever disappears — the chain doesn't forget. It *can't.*

Without privacy, "on-chain everything" is just "exposed everything."

## The analogy: on-chain value is in its plaintext era

Think about the early internet. Every page you loaded, every password you typed, traveled in **plaintext** — anyone between you and the server could read it. The web worked, but you'd never do your banking on it.

Then came TLS — HTTPS, the little lock in your browser. An invisible layer that encrypted everything in transit. Nobody signs up for HTTPS. It's just *there*, underneath the entire web. Encryption didn't become a product; it became the floor everything else stands on.

**On-chain value is in the plaintext era right now.** Everything is readable. There is no lock icon for your wallet.

What HTTPS did for the web, **Velum does for on-chain assets** — the invisible privacy layer that makes it safe to actually use.

## What Velum is

Velum is a **zero-knowledge privacy layer** for on-chain assets. It doesn't fight the openness of blockchains; it gives you a *choice* inside it. You shield an asset, move it, and the public link disappears while the proof that everything is valid stays on-chain.

## Who it's for

**For a person**, your balance and your history are yours again — and you can still prove exactly what you need to (to an auditor, an accountant, a tax authority) by handing over a view key instead of your whole life.

**For a business**, it's the difference between operating and broadcasting. Payroll, runway, and counterparties stop being public the moment you shield them — while opt-in screening and view keys keep you auditable.

**For builders**, the circuit and verifier are deployed once and shared. A wallet, payroll app, or DeFi frontend adds private transfers without running a prover or holding a key — the way a site "adds HTTPS" by pointing at a certificate instead of reinventing TLS. See [Build on Velum](/build/sdk-quickstart).
