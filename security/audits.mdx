A privacy layer you can't trust is worse than none. Velum is built to be verified, not taken on faith — open-source, non-custodial, and secured end to end.

## Security foundations

## Independent review

Velum's contracts, Groth16 verifier, circuits, and relayer are subject to independent security review. Audit reports are published here as they are completed, so you can read the findings and the remediations yourself rather than take a badge at face value.

<Note>
  Security is a program, not a milestone. Reviews, disclosures, and the trusted
  setup are ongoing and published as they land.
</Note>

## Trusted setup

Groth16 — the proof system Velum uses — is fast and cheap to verify on-chain, which is exactly why it's the right tool for a privacy pool. Its one requirement is a per-circuit **trusted setup**: a multi-party ceremony ("powers of tau" plus a circuit-specific phase-2) that produces the proving and verifying keys.

Velum's parameters come from a **multi-party ceremony**, so the setup is secure as long as **a single participant was honest** and discarded their contribution. The ceremony transcript is public, so anyone can confirm the parameters were generated correctly.

## Bug bounty & responsible disclosure

Found something? Report it privately before disclosing it publicly, and give us a reasonable window to fix it. Please don't test against other people's funds. Security contact and bug-bounty details are published alongside the audit reports.

## What we commit to

- **No custody, no master keys, no backdoors** — verifiable in the open-source code.
- **Public artifacts** — addresses, the verifier, and the ceremony transcript are all public.
- **Honest disclosure** — findings and fixes are published, not buried.
