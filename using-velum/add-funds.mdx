---
title: "Add funds (Shield)"
description: "Move an asset into your private balance."
---

Shielding deposits an asset into the Velum pool and gives you back an **Access Key** — the secret you'll use later to move or cash out those funds privately.

## Steps

<Steps>
  <Step title="Connect a wallet and pick a token">
    Choose the network and token you want to shield (native ETH/BNB or any
    supported ERC-20 — USDC, USDT, WETH, LINK). See [Multichain](/using-velum/multichain)
    for the current network list.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter an amount and shield">
    Your browser computes a commitment locally and the app submits a `shield`
    (native) or `shieldToken` (ERC-20) transaction. **No zero-knowledge proof is
    needed to deposit** — it's a single, cheap call.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save your Access Key">
    You receive an Access Key — a line of text containing the two secrets behind
    your deposit. **This is the only way to spend your funds.** Save it now.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  Your Access Key is bearer-secret: anyone who has it can spend the funds, and if
  you lose it, the funds are unrecoverable. There is no password reset — that's
  the point of a non-custodial privacy pool. Save it in a password manager and
  keep a backup.
</Warning>

## For ERC-20 tokens

ERC-20 deposits require a one-time `approve` so the pool can pull the tokens, followed by the shield transaction. The app handles the allowance check for you; you may see two wallet prompts the first time you shield a given token.

## What lands on-chain

A single `Shield` event: the contract-built commitment (leaf hash), its index in the Merkle tree, the amount, and a timestamp. The event does **not** reveal your secrets — only the commitment, which is a one-way hash. See [Architecture](/protocol/architecture) for the leaf scheme.

<Tip>
  Shielding is only half of privacy. The link is broken when you **unshield to a
  fresh address through the relayer** — not at deposit time. Read
  [The privacy model](/introduction/privacy-model) before relying on it.
</Tip>
