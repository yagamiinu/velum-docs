---
title: "Cash out (Unshield)"
description: "Withdraw your private balance to any wallet."
---

Unshielding withdraws funds from your private balance to any address, proving your note is valid **without revealing which deposit it came from**.

## Steps

<Steps>
  <Step title="Paste your Access Key">
    The secret you saved when you shielded or generated a request. Velum uses it to
    find your note in the pool and build the proof.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose a recipient">
    Enter the destination address. **Use a fresh address that has no link to your
    deposit wallet** — this is the single most important step for staying private.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Keep the relayer on">
    With the relayer enabled (default), a third party submits the withdrawal, so
    your wallet never signs or pays gas for it — nothing links the cash-out back
    to you. It charges a small fee.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Generate proof and submit">
    Your browser builds a zero-knowledge proof (~30s), then hands it to the relayer
    (or submits it yourself). The pool verifies it and pays out.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Relayer vs. self-submit

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Relayer (recommended)" icon="user-secret">
    A third party submits the transaction. Your wallet never appears on-chain.
    Costs a small relayer fee on top of the protocol fee. **This preserves your
    anonymity.**
  </Card>
  <Card title="Self-submit" icon="wallet">
    You send the transaction yourself. Cheaper (no relayer fee), but **your wallet
    is written on-chain as the sender** — which links the withdrawal to you. Only
    use this when you don't need privacy.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Warning>
  The recipient field defaults to your connected wallet for convenience. Do **not**
  accept that default if you care about privacy — cashing out to your deposit
  wallet reconnects everything. Always paste a fresh, unlinked address.
</Warning>

## Fees

- **Protocol fee** — a percentage (`feeBps`, capped at 10%) deducted from the withdrawn amount.
- **Relayer fee** — charged only when you withdraw via the relayer, to cover the gas it pays on your behalf.

Self-submit incurs no relayer fee (you pay your own gas), but sacrifices privacy.

## What lands on-chain

An `Unshield` event: the nullifier hash (marks the note spent, prevents double-withdrawal), the recipient, the amount, the fee, and the relayer address. Critically, it does **not** reveal which deposit funded the withdrawal.

<Tip>
  Availability of the relayer depends on the network. If it isn't available on
  your chain, the app falls back to self-submit and tells you so — in that case,
  consider bridging to a chain where the relayer runs before cashing out.
</Tip>
