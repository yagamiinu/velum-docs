Velum's privacy rests on a **zero-knowledge proof** that a note exists in the pool without revealing which one. The circuits are written in [Circom](https://docs.circom.io) and proven with **Groth16**.

## What the proof asserts

When you unshield, your browser proves — in zero knowledge — that:

1. You know the `nullifier` and `secret` behind some leaf `Poseidon(amount, token, Poseidon(nullifier, secret))`.
2. That leaf is a member of the Merkle tree with the given `root` (via a Merkle inclusion proof).
3. The revealed `nullifierHash` correctly corresponds to that note's `nullifier`.
4. The `amount` and `token` being withdrawn match the ones committed in the leaf.

The proof reveals the **public inputs** — `root`, `nullifierHash`, `amount`, `recipient`, `relayer`, `fee`, `token` — and nothing else. In particular, it never reveals *which* leaf you spent or the secrets behind it.

## Circuit structure

| Circuit | Role |
| --- | --- |
| `merkle.circom` | Verifies a Merkle inclusion path (depth 20) using Poseidon. |
| `unshield.circom` | Top-level circuit: recomputes the leaf, checks inclusion, derives the nullifier hash, and binds the public withdrawal parameters. |

Binding `recipient`, `relayer`, and `fee` as public inputs means a relayer **cannot alter them** — it can only submit the exact withdrawal you signed off on. This is what makes it safe to hand your proof to a third party.

## Proving in the browser

Proofs are generated **client-side** with snarkjs and the compiled circuit artifacts. Your secrets never leave your device — not to Velum, not to the relayer. Proving takes roughly 30 seconds on a typical laptop.

<Note>
  snarkjs emits the `pB` (G2) coordinates in `[c1, c0]` order, while the on-chain
  verifier expects `[c0, c1]`. Integrations that submit proofs directly must swap
  these coordinates. The relayer performs this swap server-side; the reference
  frontend swaps it for the self-submit path. See [Integrate](/build/integrate).
</Note>

## Trusted setup

Groth16 requires a per-circuit trusted setup (a "powers of tau" ceremony followed by a circuit-specific phase-2). Velum's proving and verifying keys come from a **multi-party ceremony** — secure as long as a single participant was honest and discarded their contribution — and the transcript is public. See [Security & audits](/security/audits) for details.

## Verifier

The compiled Groth16 verifier is deployed on-chain (`Groth16Verifier`) and called by the pool during `unshield`. On Solana, an equivalent on-chain `zk_verifier` program performs the same check. The verifier is fixed at deploy time; changing the circuit means deploying a new verifier.
