Velum is built on a simple conviction: **privacy and compliance are not opposites.** The goal is to make disclosure something you *grant*, not something the chain does to you by default. Compliance is built in, not bolted on.

## Sanctions screening

Screening happens at **both ends**, without anyone taking custody of funds:

- **On deposit (on-chain).** Both shield paths screen the depositing address against a `SanctionsOracle` contract. A sanctioned address cannot deposit.
- **On withdrawal (off-chain).** The [relayer](/protocol/relayer) screens the destination address against OFAC / sanctions lists before submitting the withdrawal.

Because the pool is a public contract and the relayer is permissionless, this screening is a policy the honest infrastructure enforces — not a custodial chokepoint.

## View keys — selective disclosure

The core idea from the cypherpunk manifesto: *privacy is the power to selectively reveal oneself.* View keys make that concrete.

Instead of exposing your entire on-chain history to everyone, forever, you can hand a **view key** to a specific party — an auditor, an accountant, a tax authority — that lets them verify exactly what you choose to show them. Disclosure becomes a deliberate act, scoped to one counterparty, rather than a default broadcast to the whole world.

<Note>
  For a business, this is the point: opt-in screening and view keys keep you
  auditable, so your compliance team and your privacy team finally want the same
  thing. Payroll and runway stop being public the moment you shield them, while
  you retain the ability to prove what you must.
</Note>

## What this is not

- **Not a backdoor.** No party can decrypt your activity without a view key you deliberately grant. Velum holds no master key.
- **Not custody.** Funds are only ever moved by a valid proof against the public pool contract. Neither Velum nor the relayer can seize or freeze shielded funds.
- **Not a license to evade.** Velum is not for use in jurisdictions where operating or using a privacy protocol is prohibited. See the Terms of Use and your local law.

## Posture summary

| Control | Where | Who enforces |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Sanctions screening (deposit) | On-chain | `SanctionsOracle` |
| Sanctions screening (withdrawal) | Off-chain | Relayer |
| Selective disclosure | Off-chain | You, via view keys |
| Custody | — | Nobody (non-custodial) |
