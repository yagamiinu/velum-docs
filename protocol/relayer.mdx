---
title: "Relayer"
description: "How withdrawals reach the chain without exposing your wallet."
---

The relayer is what makes a **private** withdrawal possible. Without it, you would have to submit the `unshield` transaction from your own wallet — and `msg.sender` would link the withdrawal straight back to you.

## What it does

<Steps>
  <Step title="Receives your proof">
    Your browser generates the zero-knowledge proof and POSTs it to the relayer
    with the public inputs (root, nullifier hash, amount, recipient, fee, token).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Screens the destination">
    The relayer screens the recipient address off-chain against sanctions / OFAC
    lists before submitting. On-chain screening already covered the depositor; the
    relayer covers the destination.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Submits the transaction">
    The relayer sends the `unshield` transaction, becoming `msg.sender`. It pays
    the gas and collects its fee from the withdrawn amount. Your wallet never
    appears.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Why it can't cheat

The `recipient`, `relayer`, and `fee` are **public inputs bound into the zero-knowledge proof**. If the relayer tried to change the recipient, the amount, or take a larger fee, the proof would no longer verify and the transaction would revert. The relayer is a dumb pipe — it can submit your withdrawal or refuse, but it cannot alter or steal it.

<Info>
  The relayer never sees your secrets. It only ever handles a finished proof and
  its public inputs. It cannot spend your notes, cannot link your deposit, and
  cannot reconstruct your Access Key.
</Info>

## Fees

The relayer collects a fee (bounded by the proof's `fee` input) to cover the gas it fronts. This is separate from the protocol fee. When you self-submit instead, there's no relayer fee — but you sacrifice the privacy the relayer provides.

## Availability

The relayer runs per-network. The reference frontend enables it by default where available and falls back to self-submit (with a clear warning) where it isn't. A Solana relay path exists alongside the EVM one. Anyone can run a relayer against the public pool contract — it holds no special privilege.

## Screening and compliance

The relayer is also the enforcement point for **destination screening**. Combined with the on-chain sanctions oracle on deposit, this gives Velum screening at both ends without any party holding custody of funds. See [Compliance](/protocol/compliance).
